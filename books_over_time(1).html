Analysis¶

This looks at the relationship between suicide rates over time and the number of mental health-related books published. The goal is to explore whether trends in mental health awareness, as reflected in publications, align with changes in societal suicide rates.

In [330]:
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
# include data from both previous visualizaiton and sort by year
plt.figure(figsize=(13, 6))

# Scatter plot 
plt.scatter(merged_df['YEAR'], merged_df['num_books'], color='green', alpha=1, label='Number of Books Published', s=80)  # Increased size (s=80)
plt.scatter(merged_df['YEAR'], merged_df['suicide_rate'], color='pink', alpha=0.2, label='Suicide Rate', s=80)  # Adjusted transparency for visibility

# Add labels and title
plt.xlabel('Year')
plt.ylabel('Number of Books Published / Suicide Rate (per 100,000)')  # Combined label
plt.title('Trends in Mental Health Books and Suicide Rates Over Time')

# Add legend
plt.legend()

plt.show()

Our data is clustered which means that there are specific time period that could benefit from further analysis, like looking at social context. We can however see that in some clusters when the number of books were published was higher (2010), the suicide rates were lower than we have seen. And in places where suicide rates were at its highest (70 (per 100,000) the book publication was lower (around 10 books). High suicide rates with low books published could indicate a time of low awareness about mental health, or perhaps the mental health movement was not as prevalent during that time, and suicide rates were higher because people had fewer resources and support. Showing a need for a push in awareness and mental health resources in that specific period. Low suicide rate with high books published could indicate periods of greater mental health awareness and possibly availability of resources help prevent suicide. This aligns with when there was an increas in mental health awareness campaigns in 21st. This shows the importacnce of continuing to build on the increasing mental health awareness to reduce suicide rates.
